Bidgely – Your Power Saving App (UX Case Study)


Project Objectives

Bidgely is an AI-powered energy management application for EV owners. It provides personalized charging suggestions based on real-time electricity tariffs, helping users reduce monthly bills while contributing to energy conservation. The aim was to build a simple yet efficient mobile app for smart EV charging.


Problem Statement

EV owners face challenges in optimizing their vehicle charging schedules due to fluctuating tariffs and lack of awareness. The current solutions offer limited automation and minimal energy-saving guidance. Our goal was to build an intuitive platform that automates smart charging and encourages eco-conscious behavior.

Key Requirements

  • Display real-time battery level and charge status.
  • AI-driven automated charging strategy control.
  • Show historical usage data and energy trends.
  • Enable alerts/notifications when charging is optimal.
  • Rewards system for energy-efficient behavior.

Screens included: Login, Dashboard, Usage Trend, Notification Flow, Rewards Summary.

User Research

Given the novelty of the idea, we used qualitative research to analyze potential EV user pain points. Competitor benchmarking helped us assess market gaps, while persona development captured behavioral data for decision-making.

Methods used:

  • Competitive Analysis
  • Persona Development

Key findings included user confusion over tariff times, limited app trust, and desire for seamless integration with car systems.

Methods Used:

  • Qualitative Research
  • Competitor Benchmarking
  • Persona Development

Key Findings:

  • 40% of users were unsure when to charge to save costs.
  • 30% preferred fully automated charging through AI.
  • 20% wanted better visual usage data.
  • 10% were more engaged with a rewards system.

Conclusion: These insights helped prioritize auto-scheduling, usage dashboards, and gamification for higher user engagement and retention.

Goals

  • Minimize electricity costs.
  • Automate charging during low-tariff hours.
  • Track usage patterns and savings.

Pain Points

  • Manually tracking best time to charge.
  • Lack of clear EV charging history.
  • No reward or incentive to use energy wisely.

Information Architecture

  • 1. Login / Sign-Up
  • 2. Dashboard
    • Current battery level
    • Charging control (manual/auto)
  • 3. Usage Trend
    • Energy consumption chart
    • Daily/monthly summaries
  • 4. Notifications
    • Suggested charge time
    • Click-to-activate session
  • 5. Rewards
    • Points earned
    • Redeemable offers

UI & Visual Design

We opted for a dark theme with electric green accents to align with sustainability. Minimal scrolling, card-based layouts, and high contrast were implemented to enhance usability and reduce cognitive load.

  • 3–4 tap goal completion flow.
  • No-scroll dashboard design.
  • Battery visuals + real-time indicators.

Conclusion

Bidgely bridges the gap between energy awareness and automation by offering AI-driven EV charging suggestions, a clean interface, and reward systems to boost app retention. Designed to save money while saving the planet.
